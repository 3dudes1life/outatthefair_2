The OATF legacy

One fair day became a movement.

What began as an unofficial gathering in 2011 grew into an official, award-winning fair tradition built around visibility, joy, family and belonging.

Begin the story See it in pictures

Why it mattered

Visibility inside everyday public life.

The goal was never to create another separate LGBTQ+ festival. It was to build a welcoming home base inside a mainstream county fair—where LGBTQ+ guests could enjoy the same rides, food, exhibits, concerts and traditions as everyone else while knowing there was a space created with them in mind.

That simple idea changed the relationship between fairs and the communities they serve. OATF connected fair leadership, performers, nonprofits, families and allies through a model that made inclusion part of the fair itself.

The model Inside the fair. Part of the fair.

Not separated from the midway. Not hidden in a ballroom. A visible, family-friendly gathering woven into the full fair-day experience.

2011
Origin

An unofficial gathering begins.

The first community gathering takes place at the San Diego County Fair. There is no major stage, no network and no long-term plan—just the belief that LGBTQ+ people deserved to feel visible and welcome inside one of the region’s biggest public traditions.

One fairSan Diego becomes the birthplace of the idea.

2014
Official

Out at the Fair® gets its name.

After several years of relationship-building and formal planning, the unofficial gathering becomes an official fair program with a distinct identity. Out at the Fair® is born—and the event moves from an idea into a repeatable experience.

A real brandA recognizable identity gives the program room to grow.
Fair partnershipPlanning begins in collaboration with fair leadership.
Community programmingPerformers and nonprofit partners become central to the day.
2016
Access

Inclusion expands beyond the stage.

The experience grows beyond entertainment. Gender-neutral restrooms, staff awareness and mobile engagement become part of a broader effort to make the fairgrounds feel more welcoming from arrival through closing time.

Guest experienceBelonging becomes part of the full fair journey.
Operational changeInclusion reaches facilities, staff and communications.
Family focusThe event remains joyful, visible and all-ages.
2017
Expansion

The model travels.

California State Fair and New Mexico State Fair join the growing network. OATF proves the concept is not limited to one location—it can adapt to different fairgrounds while keeping the same core promise of community, visibility and fun.

New marketsThe experience reaches guests beyond Southern California.
Repeatable frameworkThe fair-within-the-fair model becomes portable.
Stronger networkMore fairs begin learning from the original partnership.
2018
Recognition

Five fairs—and a major industry honor.

Marin and Santa Clara join the network, and the Western Fairs Association recognizes Out at the Fair with the Barham Award for innovation and impact. What started as a community gathering is now influencing the fair industry itself.

Five locationsThe network reaches a new level of scale.
Barham AwardIndustry recognition validates the model and its impact.
National attentionOATF becomes a reference point for fair inclusion.
2019
Growth

Orange County joins the tradition.

Out at the Fair arrives at the OC Fair, extending the Southern California footprint and bringing the experience to another major regional fairground.

New audienceOrange County families discover the OATF experience.
Regional strengthThe Southern California network becomes even more visible.
More memoriesNew stages, performers and traditions take shape.
2021
Evolution

The mission gets a larger home.

OutAt Inc. becomes the producing company, creating a broader structure for the brand, its partnerships and the next phase of the mission.

New structureThe organization evolves around the growing work.
