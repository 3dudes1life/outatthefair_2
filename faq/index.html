Frequently asked questions

Everything to know before fair day.

Event details vary by fair, but the answers below explain how the Out at the Fair experience usually works.

Is Out at the Fair a separate ticketed LGBTQ+ festival?

Usually, no. OATF is designed to be part of the host fair and is generally included with fair admission. A specific add-on activity may require a separate ticket, and that will be clearly noted.

Is the event family-friendly?

Yes. Entertainment and activities are created for an all-ages fair environment. Programming may include live music, hosts, community booths, story time, games and a glam show presented within fair standards.

Do I need to stay at the OATF stage all day?

No. The stage is a home base, not a separate venue. Guests are encouraged to explore the entire fair and return whenever they want entertainment, connection or a familiar space.

When will 2027 dates and schedules be posted?

Each fair page will be updated as agreements, dates, stages, schedules and ticket information are confirmed.

How do performers or community organizations participate?

Use the Participate page for current application information and deadlines. Opportunities vary by fair and season.

What accessibility services are available?

Accessibility services are generally provided through the host fairgrounds. Guests should review the accessibility page for the specific fair and contact the fair directly for mobility, sensory or personal-care accommodations.

Can another fair bring Out at the Fair to its grounds?

Yes. Fair executives and event partners can contact OATF to discuss licensing, programming, production and partnership options.

Still have a question?

Reach the Out at the Fair team for general, media or partnership inquiries.
