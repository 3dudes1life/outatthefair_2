The model that changed fair culture

A safe home base inside a place everyone already loves.

The Out at the Fair difference

We never wanted to pull the community away from the fair.

Out at the Fair was built to place LGBTQ+ visibility directly inside a mainstream, all-ages fairground experience. Guests can enjoy rides, food, exhibits and concerts, then return to an OATF stage and community space whenever they want connection, entertainment or a familiar face.

Three-way partnership

Fair. Community. OATF.

The strongest events happen when the host fair, local organizations and Out at the Fair each bring their strengths. That triangle creates visibility, resources and a welcoming stage without turning the community celebration into a separate room or premium add-on.

Quick answers

What guests and fair partners ask most.

Is Out at the Fair a separate ticketed LGBTQ+ festival?

Usually, no. OATF programming is designed to be part of the host fair and is generally included with fair admission unless a specific add-on activity says otherwise.

Is the event family-friendly?

Yes. Out at the Fair is intentionally built for an all-ages fair environment, with entertainment, story time, community partners, games and programming designed for families, friends and allies.

When will 2027 dates be announced?

Each fair page will be updated as dates, stages, schedules and ticket information are confirmed with the host fair.
